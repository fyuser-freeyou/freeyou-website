# FreeYou — Social Media Strategy
**Phase 1: Pre-launch to First 500 Members**
**June 2026 — December 2026**

---

## The One Rule

Every piece of content must do one of three things:
1. **Educate** a freelancer about something they didn't know (and now trust FreeYou for knowing)
2. **Build the community** (show people belong here)
3. **Convert** (drive someone to apply)

If a piece of content doesn't do at least one of these, don't post it.

## Platform Strategy

### LinkedIn — Primary channel (highest ROI for Phase 1)
**Why:** Indian skilled freelancers, tech founders, potential Studio clients, and investors all live here. Organic reach is still possible. Thought leadership converts.

**Account:** FreeYou company page + Ronald's personal profile (more important than the company page in Phase 1)

**Ronald's personal LinkedIn strategy:**
Post as a founder, not as a brand. "I'm building FreeYou because..." Personal, honest, with learnings. Founders with authentic voices outperform brand pages 10:1.

**Posting frequency:** 3–4x per week (Ronald personal) + 2x per week (company page)

**Content mix:**
- 40% Education (freelancing rights, legal tips, financial advice for Indian freelancers)
- 30% Community (member spotlights, behind-the-scenes, founder updates)
- 20% Provocation (takes on the freelance economy, "why marketplaces are broken")
- 10% Product/announcements

**Post formats that work on LinkedIn:**
- Text-only "carousel alternative": Short story with line breaks, ends with a CTA
- Native document/PDF carousel: 5-8 slides, "swipe" content
- Single image with bold text overlay
- Short-form video (60-90 seconds, subtitled) — best reach currently
- Poll: "What's your biggest pain as a freelancer?" (qualitative research + engagement)

### WhatsApp — Highest-convert channel (don't ignore)
**Why:** Indian users open WhatsApp 23+ times/day. Broadcast lists = 100% open rate (vs. 20% email). Best channel for waitlist nurture.

**Setup:**
- **"FreeYou Insiders" broadcast list** — opt-in for waitlist members. Max 256 per list.
- **WhatsApp Community** — once you have 50+ members, create a community with sub-groups by skill type
- **Status updates** — regular brand-consistent status updates (product updates, tips, announcements)

**Broadcast message cadence:**
- Week 1 after opt-in: Welcome + what to expect
- Week 2: Educational tip ("Did you know your Aadhaar can verify your professional identity?")
- Week 3: Member story or product update
- Week 4: Gentle nudge to complete onboarding / share with a freelancer friend

**Tone on WhatsApp:** Conversational, not broadcast-y. Write like you're texting a friend.

## Content Pillars (Cross-platform)

### Pillar 1: "Know Your Rights" (Education)
Indian freelancers are unaware of their legal rights. This positions FreeYou as the trusted authority.

Examples:
- "Your client can't change the scope after signing. Here's what Indian law says."
- "The one clause every Indian freelancer must have in their contract"
- "What is Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act and why freelancers need to know it"
- "DPDP Act: What it means for freelancers handling client data"

---

### Pillar 2: "Real Numbers" (Authenticity + trust)
Show the actual financial reality of Indian freelancing — unfiltered.

Examples:
- "₹1,20,000/month freelancer in Bangalore: breakdown of income, expenses, taxes"
- "How Indian banks treat freelance income (and how to fix it)"
- "The real cost of not using a contract: one freelancer's ₹2.4 lakh story"
- "₹1,499/month: Is FreeYou worth it? Let's do the math."

---

### Pillar 3: "Not a Marketplace" (Positioning / provocation)
Challenge the status quo. Build an enemy (the exploitative marketplace model).

Examples:
- "Upwork takes 20% of your fee. FreeYou takes 0%."
- "You are not a commodity. Stop being priced like one."
- "The problem with every freelance platform (and why we built something different)"
- "Who actually benefits when you get more clients? Hint: not you."

---

### Pillar 4: "Community" (Social proof + belonging)
Show that real people are choosing FreeYou. Community is the product.

Examples:
- Member spotlights: "Meet Priya — UI designer, Bangalore, 4 years freelancing"
- City chapter updates: "Bangalore chapter: 30 members and growing"
- Behind the scenes: "Our first cohort call. Here's what we learned."
- User-generated: Re-share members' posts about their FreeYou experience

---

### Pillar 5: "Build in Public" (Ronald's personal content)
Ronald documenting the journey — decisions, learnings, numbers. Indian startup Twitter loves this.

Examples:
- "Month 1: 23 applications. 14 calls. 8 paid members. Here's what I learned."
- "The decision to make FreeYou a subscription, not a marketplace (thread)"
- "I almost hired a CTO last week. Here's why I didn't."
- "What 50 freelancers told me about why they don't use contracts"

## Engagement Rules

**The 1-hour rule:** Within 1 hour of posting, engage with every comment. The algorithm rewards early engagement. The community feels heard.

**The reply rule:** Reply to every DM that isn't spam. In Phase 1, every person who reaches out is a potential member.

**The mention rule:** When someone mentions pain points about freelancing (late payments, bad clients, no contracts) — reply with empathy + awareness of FreeYou. Not a hard sell. Just: "This is exactly why we're building FreeYou. Are you based in India?"

**The reshare rule:** When a member posts about their work, their project, their win — reshare it. Every member reshare = 10x the loyalty.

**The comment rule:** Comment on 5-10 posts per day from Indian freelancers, startup founders, and Indian fintech content creators. Genuine comments, not "Great post!" Value-first engagement.
